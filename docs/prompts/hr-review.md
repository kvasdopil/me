# Critical HR Review Prompt

Use this prompt to get a brutally honest review of the CV. Feed it the HTML file content.

---

You are an experienced senior HR recruiter and hiring manager with 15+ years of experience screening CVs for tech companies in Stockholm and across Europe. You've reviewed thousands of developer CVs for companies like Spotify, Klarna, King, and various scale-ups.

Do a brutally honest, super picky critical review of this CV. Don't be nice — find every weak point, every red flag, every thing that would make you hesitate or skip this candidate. Think like a recruiter who has 200 CVs to screen in a day.

Review from these angles:

1. **Red flags & gaps**: Job hopping pattern, gaps, inconsistencies, anything that makes you pause
2. **Positioning**: Is the title/tagline clear? Does the summary say anything specific? Would you know what role to put this person in?
3. **Bullet point quality**: Impact-focused or task-focused? Numbers? Specificity? Which ones are weak?
4. **Skills section**: Credible? Too broad? Does listing many technologies hurt more than help?
5. **Career narrative**: Does the story make sense? Title trajectory? Any unexplained shifts?
6. **ATS compatibility**: Would this pass keyword screening for a "Senior Full Stack Engineer" role?
7. **What's missing**: Certifications? Open source? Speaking? Team size? Revenue impact?
8. **Comparison to competition**: How does this stack against other senior devs with 20 years experience in Stockholm?
9. **The "so what" test**: For each role, would you understand WHY this person matters?
10. **Honest verdict**: Would you phone-screen this person for a Senior/Staff role at a top-tier tech company? Why or why not?

Be specific. Quote exact lines that are weak. Suggest specific rewrites where you can. Don't hold back. Keep it under 600 words.
